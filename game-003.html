Q1
Quarter 1 @influenceisinput
Your inputs shape your output.

The third business lesson

In basketball, influence is an asset — or a liability.

The people, voices, habits, music, feeds, coaches, teammates, and environments around you are not neutral. They are training you. The question is: training you for what?

Your circle is either building your standard or negotiating it down.
Q2
Quarter 2 @yourcirclecounts
The circle becomes the standard.

Influence becomes behavior

Who you listen to changes how you move.

If your circle normalizes excuses, you will defend excuses. If your circle normalizes work, you will feel lazy when you are not working. Influence changes what feels normal.

People

Who has access to your decisions, confidence, attitude, and time?

Content

What are you watching, hearing, and copying every day?

Standards

What does your environment call normal?

Opportunities

Who puts you closer to better rooms, better habits, and better decisions?

HT
Halftime @notallattentionhelps
A warning before the second half.

Not every voice deserves access

Some people clap for your talent but pull down your discipline.

Basketball business rule: access to your attention is access to your future.
Q3
Quarter 3 @influencemap
Map the voices.

Your influence map

Every player needs to know who is shaping the way they think.

Influence can come from a coach, parent, friend, trainer, teammate, brand, fan, algorithm, or even a rival. The danger is not influence. The danger is unconscious influence.

Who raises your standard?

These are the people who make you want to prepare better.

Who drains your focus?

These are the people or feeds that leave you distracted, negative, or lazy.

Who tells you the truth?

Truth is not always comfortable, but it protects your value.

Who opens better rooms?

Some influence connects you to opportunity, wisdom, and better competition.

Q4
Quarter 4 @chooseyourinputs
Build the environment on purpose.

How to upgrade your influence

Do not wait for a better circle. Start becoming the standard.

Influence is not only what affects you. It is also what you become for others. A serious player does not just look for better energy — they bring it.

1
Audit your circle.

Name who is helping your value and who is lowering your standard.

2
Protect your attention.

What you give attention to starts shaping your decisions.

3
Follow better examples.

Study people who live the standards you claim to want.

4
Become good influence.

The strongest players raise the room without needing attention.
