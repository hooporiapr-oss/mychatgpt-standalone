Q1
Quarter 1 @seethedoor
The prepared mind sees more.

The ninth business lesson

Opportunity is everywhere, but not everyone is trained to recognize it.

Some players only see the big stage. Prepared players see the small signs before the big stage appears: a conversation, a workout, a role, a relationship, a tournament, a need, or a door opening quietly.

The prepared mind sees what the unprepared mind misses.
Q2
Quarter 2 @threekeys
Three keys to opportunity.

Awareness. Readiness. Action.

Opportunity mindset has three keys: awareness, readiness, and action.

If you lack awareness, you miss the door. If you lack readiness, you waste the door. If you lack action, you watch someone else walk through it.

1. Awareness

Notice what others ignore. Pay attention to needs, patterns, people, rooms, and timing.

2. Readiness

Be prepared before the opportunity arrives. Proof and habits make you believable.

3. Action

Move when the door opens. Hesitation can close what preparation created.

HT
Halftime @dontwait
A warning before the second half.

Do not wait for opportunity

Waiting is not a strategy if you are not preparing while you wait.

Basketball business rule: free time becomes future opportunity when you use it to prepare.
Q3
Quarter 3 @recognizesigns
Recognize the signs.

Opportunity shows signs first

Big doors usually start as small details.

A coach asking one question. A teammate needing help. A brand watching quietly. A local event needing leadership. A young player asking for guidance. A gym needing energy. Details create direction.

Look for needs

Opportunity often starts where someone needs help, leadership, trust, or energy.

Look for rooms

Some rooms are small today but connect to bigger rooms later.

Look for timing

The same action at the right time can open a different level.

Look for proof gaps

When proof is missing, building it can become your opportunity.

Q4
Quarter 4 @takeaction
Move when the door opens.

Act with confidence

Opportunity mindset means you prepare before, recognize during, and act when the moment arrives.

1
Prepare daily.

Do not wait for the door to open before you start becoming ready.

2
Stay open.

Closed minds miss doors because the opportunity does not look expected.

3
Move fast.

You do not need to be reckless, but you do need to respond.

4
Follow through.

The player who follows through becomes easier to trust next time.
