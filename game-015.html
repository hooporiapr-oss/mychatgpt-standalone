Q1
Quarter 1 @pressuretest
Pressure tests recall.

The fifteenth business lesson

A player does not need perfect memory. A player needs usable memory under pressure.

In quiet moments, many players know the plan. Under pressure, only trained recall stays available. That is the difference between knowing and executing.

Pressure does not ask what you know. Pressure asks what you can access fast.
Q2
Quarter 2 @orderinchaos
Keep order in chaos.

From order to chaos

COG 02 trained order. Game 015 asks: can you keep the order when the moment gets messy?

The game gets messy when the defense rotates, the clock drops, the crowd gets loud, the ball moves fast, and the first option disappears.

Remember the role

Know what your job is before the possession breaks down.

Remember the space

Know where pressure is, where help is, and where the next opening can appear.

Remember the order

Do not skip the simple read because the moment feels urgent.

Remember the next move

The best players are ready for the next action before the first one ends.

Q3
Quarter 3 @pressurememorysystem
Build a pressure memory system.

How to protect recall

Pressure recall improves when the player builds simple anchors.

An anchor is a simple cue that helps the mind return to the right action: scan, space, order, option, execute.

Scan

Find the information before pressure chooses for you.

Space

Know where you are and where the advantage can form.

Order

Do the right things in the right sequence.

Execute

Turn memory into action before the moment closes.

Q4
Quarter 4 @recallchecklist
The pressure recall checklist.

Pressure recall checklist

A serious player trains recall before pressure arrives.

1
Review the plan before speed.

If the order is not clear slow, it will not be clear fast.

2
Train memory with pressure.

Add speed, noise, time, and consequence to recall training.

3
Use simple cues.

Short cues protect the mind when the game becomes chaotic.

4
Recover quickly after mistakes.

The next play still needs your memory, focus, and decision.
