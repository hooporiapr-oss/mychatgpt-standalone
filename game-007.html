Q1
Quarter 1 @proofbeatsclaims
Proof beats claims.

The seventh business lesson

In basketball, proof is what turns belief into trust.

Everyone can say they work hard. Everyone can say they are serious. Everyone can say they deserve an opportunity. Proof is what separates the player who talks from the player people trust.

Potential gets attention. Proof gets trusted.
Q2
Quarter 2 @documentyourwork
Document your work.

Documentation creates proof

If nobody can see the work, the work is harder to believe.

Documentation is not showing off. Documentation is evidence. It helps coaches, teams, sponsors, parents, trainers, brands, and future partners understand what you are building.

Work sessions

Show that you are putting in consistent work, not random effort.

Game film

Film gives people something real to evaluate.

Stats and results

Numbers do not tell everything, but they can support the story.

Growth evidence

Show how you are improving, not just where you are today.

HT
Halftime @notjustcontent
A warning before the second half.

Proof is not fake content

Do not confuse proof with pretending.

Basketball business rule: proof must be real. Fake confidence gets exposed. Real evidence gets respected.
Q3
Quarter 3 @showresults
Make results visible.

Make proof visible

Proof creates value when the right people can understand it.

A highlight is not always enough. A coach may need film clips. A sponsor may need your story. A trainer may need progress data. A parent may need discipline. A team may need reliability.

For coaches

Show execution, coachability, decision-making, and consistency.

For teams

Show fit, role value, reliability, and impact.

For sponsors

Show image, behavior, audience, story, and trust.

For yourself

Show progress so you stop guessing and start improving.

Q4
Quarter 4 @evidencelog
Build the evidence log.

Create your proof system

Do not wait until someone asks for proof. Build it before the door opens.

1
Track your work.

Keep a simple record of training, games, film, recovery, and goals.

2
Save your evidence.

Film clips, stats, feedback, progress screenshots, and testimonials matter.

3
Organize it.

Proof loses power when nobody can understand it quickly.

4
Use it with purpose.

The goal is not to spam people. The goal is to help them believe.
