💬 Game 002 🔁 Habits 🔥 Accountability
Q1
Quarter 1 @repeatisproof
What you repeat becomes your reputation.

The second business lesson

In basketball, habits are business evidence.

Coaches, teams, sponsors, and brands do not only listen to what you say. They study what you repeat. Your repeated actions tell them if you are serious, reliable, and ready.

Talent can get you noticed. Habits make people trust you.
Q2
Quarter 2 @dailydeposit
Every habit is a deposit.

Habits are deposits

Your habits are deposits into your basketball account.

Every workout, every film session, every recovery choice, every honest rep, every good decision adds proof. Not all deposits are seen immediately, but they compound.

Training habits

How often do you actually work on the things you say matter?

Body habits

Sleep, food, recovery, and preparation affect your basketball value.

Mind habits

Your focus, response to correction, and emotional control are part of your product.

Communication habits

How you talk, listen, ask, and respond changes how people value you.

HT
Halftime @wishisnotwork
A warning before the second half.

Wanting it is not the same as working for it

A lot of players want the result. Fewer players want the routine.

Basketball business rule: your dream creates interest, but your habits create evidence.
Q3
Quarter 3 @invisiblework
Invisible work becomes visible value.

The invisible work

Your habits show up before the scoreboard does.

The market may see the game. The coach sees the week. Your teammates see the pattern. Your body feels the routine. Your future receives the result.

Before practice

Do you arrive prepared or do you need the room to wake you up?

During practice

Do you compete with purpose or just survive the session?

After practice

Do you review, recover, and improve — or disappear until the next run?

Under pressure

Your habits decide faster than your feelings.

Q4
Quarter 4 @buildthepattern
Design the player you want to become.

How to build a basketball habit system

Do not only set goals. Build a system that makes the goal believable.

A goal says where you want to go. A habit system shows how you are going to get there.

1
Choose one habit.

Start small enough that you can repeat it.

2
Attach it to a time.

A habit needs a place in your day.

3
Track the proof.

If you do not track it, you will guess. Guessing is not accountability.

4
Repeat before you add.

Do not build a complicated plan you will quit.
