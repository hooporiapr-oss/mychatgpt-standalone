Q1
Quarter 1 @fastcleanchoices
Fast does not mean careless.

The fourteenth business lesson

Decision speed is the ability to choose clearly before pressure takes over.

A player can see the space, recognize the pattern, and still lose the play by waiting too long. The next level is choosing fast enough to use the advantage.

A good read becomes valuable when the decision arrives on time.
Q2
Quarter 2 @seechooseexecute
See. Choose. Execute.

The decision chain

The best players simplify the moment into a decision chain.

Decision speed improves when the player knows what to look for, what options matter, and what action must come next.

1. See it

Scan the floor, the defense, the pressure, and the open space.

2. Read it

Recognize what the pattern is telling you before it fully develops.

3. Decide it

Choose the best option without freezing in the middle of the play.

4. Execute it

Turn the decision into action before the advantage closes.

HT
Halftime @rushingisnotreading
Rushing is not decision speed.

Fast is not frantic

Bad speed creates panic. Good speed creates control.

Basketball business rule: the goal is not to do everything fast. The goal is to make the right decision fast enough.
Q3
Quarter 3 @optionsunderpressure
Options under pressure.

Decision speed needs options

You cannot decide quickly if you do not know your options.

Fast decisions come from preparation. The player who knows the first option, second option, and emergency option can stay calm when the game speeds up.

First option

The best read if the defense gives you the advantage immediately.

Second option

The next read when the first option closes.

Emergency option

The safe decision when pressure removes the clean play.

Business option

The move that protects trust, timing, value, and the next opportunity.

Q4
Quarter 4 @decisionspeedhabits
Build decision habits.

Decision speed checklist

A serious player trains the mind to choose cleanly under pressure.

1
Know the read before the ball arrives.

Decision speed starts before the catch.

2
Limit hesitation.

The longer you freeze, the smaller the advantage becomes.

3
Train simple options.

Simple reads become fast decisions when they are repeated.

4
Review slow decisions.

Film can show where the decision was late, forced, or unclear.
