Q1
Quarter 1 @valuebeforemoney
Money follows value.

The first business lesson

In basketball, money does not chase noise. Money chases value.

A player can have talent and still not be valuable to a coach, team, sponsor, program, league, or brand. Talent gets attention. Value creates trust.

Talent says: look at me. Value says: you can count on me.
Q2
Quarter 2 @whatisyourproduct
Every player is a product.

The player product

Your product is not just your jump shot.

Your basketball product is the full package people experience when they deal with you: skill, decisions, attitude, consistency, communication, preparation, and trust.

Skill

What can you actually do on the court?

Trust

Can coaches and teammates depend on you?

Consistency

Do you bring the same standard when nobody is clapping?

Visibility

Can the right people see what you bring?

HT
Halftime @attentionisnotvalue
A warning before the second half.

Attention is not the same as value

Views are not value. Hype is not value. Being known is not the same as being trusted.

Basketball business rule: attention may open the door, but value is what keeps you in the room.
Q3
Quarter 3 @basketballmarket
Who is buying what you bring?

The basketball market

The business of basketball is about solving problems for the people who choose players.

A coach needs trust. A team needs fit. A league needs quality. A sponsor needs image. A fan needs connection. A brand needs belief. Your value changes depending on who is looking.

To a coach

Value means trust, execution, discipline, and fit.

To a team

Value means helping the group win, not just helping yourself look good.

To a sponsor

Value means image, behavior, story, audience, and trust.

To your future

Value means using basketball to build options, not just memories.

Q4
Quarter 4 @provevalue
Value must be shown.

How value is proven

You do not prove value by saying you are serious. You prove it by patterns.

The business of basketball watches patterns: how you train, how you respond, how you communicate, how you prepare, how you recover, and how you handle pressure.

1
Show up.

Availability is part of value.

2
Be coachable.

Correction is not disrespect. It is information.

3
Do the invisible work.

Your habits speak before your highlights do.

4
Make people better.

A valuable player raises the room.

Game 001 Review

Before Game 002, answer this honestly: what is your current basketball value?

1. What do people trust you to do?

Do not answer with what you hope. Answer with what you have shown.

2. What problem do you solve for a coach?

Scoring is one answer. It is not the only answer.

3. What makes you dependable?

Dependability is business value.

4. What part of your value needs proof?

The market does not believe everything you say. It believes what you can show.

5. Why would someone choose you?

This is the question that starts Basketball Money.
