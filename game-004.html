💬 Game 004 🔁 Opportunity 🔥 Readiness
Q1
Quarter 1 @seeitfirst
First, you have to see it.

The fourth business lesson

In basketball, opportunity is not always loud.

Sometimes opportunity looks like extra reps, a new role, a coach watching quietly, a teammate needing help, a small tournament, a conversation, or a chance to prove trust.

If you only recognize big doors, you will miss the small ones that open them.
Q2
Quarter 2 @createit
Some opportunities are built.

Opportunity can be created

Do not wait for someone to discover you. Build reasons to be seen.

Opportunity grows when value becomes visible. A player creates opportunity by improving, documenting, showing up, communicating, building relationships, and making it easier for the right people to trust them.

Build skill

Opportunity is easier to claim when your work is real.

Build visibility

The right people cannot choose what they cannot see.

Build relationships

Relationships carry trust faster than noise does.

Build proof

Proof makes your opportunity believable.

HT
Halftime @readyornot
A warning before the second half.

Opportunity without readiness becomes regret

The door can open and still expose that you were not ready.

Basketball business rule: opportunity is not the finish line. Opportunity is the test.
Q3
Quarter 3 @takeit
Confidence must become action.

Taking the opportunity

When opportunity appears, hesitation can cost you.

Taking an opportunity does not mean being reckless. It means being prepared enough to move when the moment asks for courage, discipline, communication, and execution.

Speak up

Sometimes opportunity starts with asking the right question.

Step in

When your number is called, do not act surprised that the moment arrived.

Follow through

Opportunity respects the player who finishes what they start.

Learn fast

Every opportunity teaches you how to handle the next one better.

Q4
Quarter 4 @ownit
Turn opportunity into freedom.

Owning the opportunity

The goal is not just to get one chance. The goal is to become someone opportunities can trust.

When players handle opportunity with maturity, they earn more rooms, more trust, more visibility, and more choices.

Game 004 Review

Before Game 005, answer this honestly: are you waiting for opportunity or building it?

1. What opportunity is already near you?

Look for small doors: people, practices, leagues, conversations, roles, or projects.

2. What opportunity do you need to create?

Do not wait to be picked if there is a way to build value now.

3. What proof would make you more ready?

Opportunity trusts players who can show preparation.

4. What action can you take this week?

Make it specific. A message, a workout, a video, a meeting, a tryout, or a follow-up.

5. Can you see it, create it, and take it?

This is the question that closes Game 004.
