Q1
Quarter 1 @relationshipsopenrooms
Relationships open rooms.

The tenth business lesson

In basketball, connections are not shortcuts. They are bridges.

A real connection is not using people. A real connection is building trust, giving value, listening first, following through, and becoming someone people are willing to recommend.

People open doors for people they know, like, trust, and believe will follow through.
Q2
Quarter 2 @buildrealconnections
Build better connections.

How to build real connections

The strongest connections are built through value, not noise.

Players sometimes chase attention before they build trust. But the relationship that creates opportunity usually begins with respect, consistency, and value.

Be real

Authenticity attracts the right people and filters out the wrong rooms.

Listen first

Listening helps you understand before you ask.

Give value

Look for ways to help first, not for ways to get first.

Stay consistent

Relationships grow with consistent communication and repeated trust.

Follow through

Do what you said you would do. Reputation is currency.

Halftime @dontnetworkfake

Do not network fake

People can feel when you only show up because you want something.

Basketball business rule: weak connections ask before giving. Strong connections give value, build trust, and create options over time.
Q3
Quarter 3 @networkmap
Know your network.

Types of connections

Different connections create different options.

A serious player learns to recognize the role of mentors, peers, coaches, industry people, community leaders, brands, trainers, event organizers, and former players.

Mentors

Guide your growth and help you avoid mistakes.

Peers

Grow with you, challenge you, and share rooms with you.

Coaches

Open levels through trust, role clarity, and recommendation.

Industry

Creates access to events, brands, training, media, leagues, and business opportunities.

Community

Builds your platform, reputation, and impact beyond the court.

Q4
Quarter 4 @connectionchecklist
Build today. Access tomorrow.

Daily connection checklist

Connections create options when you treat relationships like a responsibility.

1
Who can I learn from today?

Learning makes the relationship valuable before you ask for anything.

2
Who can I help today?

Helping builds trust faster than talking about yourself.

3
Who can I follow up with today?

Follow-up shows maturity, respect, and seriousness.

4
How can I add value today?

Your network grows when people associate your name with value.
