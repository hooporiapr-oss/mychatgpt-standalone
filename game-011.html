Q1
Quarter 1 @remembertheplan
Remember the plan.

The eleventh business lesson

Basketball is sequence. Business is sequence. Growth is sequence.

A player does not only need talent. A player needs to remember what comes next: the play, the read, the role, the assignment, the follow-up, and the next move.

Memory becomes power when it turns into correct action.
Q2
Quarter 2 @ordercreatescontrol
Order creates control.

Why order matters

The right action in the wrong order can still break the play.

Some players know what to do, but lose the order under pressure. That is why sequence matters. Order turns information into execution.

In the game

Remember the set, the read, the rotation, the timing, and the next pass.

In training

Repeat the correct order until it becomes automatic under pressure.

In business

Know the sequence: build value, create proof, connect, follow up, execute.

In opportunity

Remember who opened the door, what was said, and what you promised next.

HT
Halftime @pressuretestssequence
Pressure tests memory.

Pressure reveals recall

Under pressure, the player does not rise to memory they never trained.

Basketball business rule: pressure does not create recall. Pressure reveals whether recall was trained.
Q3
Quarter 3 @lockitin
Lock it in.

How to lock a sequence

Sequence memory improves when the player slows down enough to encode, then speeds up enough to execute.

You cannot recall what you never locked in. The best players train their eyes, their order, their timing, and their next move.

Watch

Pay attention before reacting. The first job is to see the whole pattern.

Chunk

Break a long sequence into smaller pieces your brain can hold.

Repeat

Repetition turns fragile memory into stronger recall.

Execute

Memory has to become action, not just information.

Q4
Quarter 4 @sequencehabits
Build sequence habits.

Sequence habits

A serious player builds habits that protect memory under pressure.

1
Repeat instructions back.

Confirm the sequence before execution.

2
Write the plan down.

Written plans protect memory when the day gets noisy.

3
Review before pressure.

Do not wait until game speed to remember the order.

4
Connect memory to action.

The goal is not to remember only. The goal is to execute correctly.

Before Game 012, answer this honestly: where do you lose the sequence?

1. What sequence do you need to remember better?

A play, a daily system, a follow-up process, a training plan, or an opportunity path.

Speed, nerves, distraction, poor preparation, or lack of repetition.

3. What system can protect your memory?

Notes, checklists, film study, repetition, reminders, or pre-game routines.

4. What should you train in COG 02?

Accuracy, longer sequence, faster recall, or fewer wrong taps.
