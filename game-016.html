Q1
Quarter 1 @scanfullfloor
Scan the full floor.

The sixteenth business lesson

A player with tunnel vision sees one thing. A prepared player sees the full situation.

In basketball, one target is not enough. You need to see the defender, the help, the cutter, the shooter, the space, the clock, and the next option.

The player who scans more sees the answer earlier.
Q2
Quarter 2 @multiplesignals
Multiple signals matter.

What multi-target vision sees

The game rarely gives one clean signal. It gives several signals at once.

The player has to organize what is important, ignore the noise, and find the correct targets before pressure changes the picture.

Primary target

The most obvious read, action, or opportunity in the moment.

Secondary target

The next option if the first target closes.

Hidden target

The quiet advantage that appears away from the ball or away from attention.

Business target

The relationship, opening, audience, or brand signal that needs follow-up.

HT
Halftime @notunnelvision
Tunnel vision is expensive.

Do not lock on one target too early

When you lock on one thing, you may miss the better thing.

Basketball business rule: attention is a weapon, but narrow attention can become a trap.
Q3
Quarter 3 @findtheflash
Find the flash.

The Reflex mindset

COG 03 trains the brain to see several targets, remember them, and find them fast.

The order no longer matters. What matters is whether the player can recognize the full group and find all correct targets before the moment disappears.

Scan wide

Do not stare at one spot. Train your eyes to capture the whole picture.

Store fast

Remember the targets before they disappear.

Find clean

Tap the correct targets without wasting touches on noise.

Reset fast

If you miss, reset the mind and prepare for the next flash.

Q4
Quarter 4 @visionhabits
Build vision habits.

Multi-target vision checklist

A serious player trains the eyes to collect more useful information.

1
Scan before acting.

Do not make the first move before seeing the full picture.

2
Identify the group.

Find all important targets, not just the loudest one.

3
Avoid noise touches.

Wrong targets waste time, points, energy, and opportunity.

4
Recover after misses.

The next flash still needs your attention.
