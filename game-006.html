Q1
Quarter 1 @moneyfollowsvalue
Money follows value.

The sixth business lesson

Money in basketball begins before the payment.

Before anyone pays, signs, sponsors, invites, promotes, or invests, they ask one silent question: is this player worth trusting?

Money is not the first reward. Trust is. Money usually follows trust.
Q2
Quarter 2 @yourvalueaccount
Your value account matters.

Your basketball value account

Every player has a value account. Your actions either deposit or withdraw.

Skill deposits. Discipline deposits. Being coachable deposits. Showing up deposits. Bad decisions withdraw. Excuses withdraw. Poor communication withdraws. No proof withdraws.

Deposits

Work, proof, consistency, relationships, discipline, and trust.

Withdrawals

Drama, excuses, poor habits, bad timing, bad attitude, and no follow-through.

Balance

Your basketball value is the balance people feel when they evaluate you.

Halftime @moneyexposes
A warning before the second half.

Money exposes habits

Money does not fix a weak system. It exposes it.

Basketball business rule: if you cannot manage attention, habits, and trust, money will only make the weakness louder.
Q3
Quarter 3 @basketballincome
Basketball money has lanes.

Where basketball money can come from

Basketball money is bigger than a paycheck from playing.

A player can create value through playing, training, coaching, camps, content, clinics, branding, sponsorship, events, community, education, and relationships.

Playing lane

Teams, leagues, tournaments, roles, contracts, and performance opportunities.

Knowledge lane

Training, teaching, coaching, clinics, camps, and player development.

Brand lane

Content, audience, sponsors, partnerships, image, and community trust.

Network lane

People, rooms, introductions, events, and relationships that create future opportunity.

Q4
Quarter 4 @moneydiscipline
Build the money system.

How to build money mindset

A serious player does not just chase money. They build the system that makes money possible.

1
Know your value.

Be clear on what problem you solve and who benefits from it.

2
Build proof.

Document work, results, growth, consistency, and impact.

3
Protect trust.

Trust is the bridge between value and money.

4
Create opportunities.

Do not only wait to be paid. Build reasons people want to work with you.
